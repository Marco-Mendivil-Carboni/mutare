% BEGINNING OF MAIN ABSTRACT TEXT

Natural populations rarely evolve under stable conditions. In this study, we introduce a minimal stochastic model to explore how adaptation unfolds under fluctuating environments and demographic constraints. By representing both environmental transitions and population dynamics as continuous-time Markov processes, we quantify how different regimes of variability shape evolutionary outcomes. Our results show that under strong environmental uncertainty and population pressure, populations evolve toward ``safer'' strategies, sacrificing part of their reproductive potential to reduce extinction risk. These findings offer a mechanistic perspective on how environmental noise and ecological limits drive the evolution of phenotypic diversity and robustness.
